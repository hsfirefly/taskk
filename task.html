Oguri Cap

Cinderella gray

Oguri Cap (Japanese: オグリキャップ, born April 27, 1985) is a retired Japanese Thoroughbred racehorse and sire. He was one of the most popular racehorses in Japan during the late 1980s and early 1990s, known for his distinctive white blaze and exceptional racing ability.

Oguri Cap was foaled at the Shadai Farm in Hokkaido, Japan. He was sired by Dancing

Oguri Cap had a successful racing career, winning several major races including the Arima Kinen, Yasuda Kinen, and Mile Championship. He is also featured as a character in the multimedia franchise "Uma Musume: Pretty Derby," where he is portrayed as a horse girl with a strong will and humble personality.

  • Name: Oguri Cap
  • Born: April 27, 1985
  • Died: March 13, 2010

  • Owner: Toyomitsu Hirai
  • Trainer: Shoichi Matsumoto
  • Record: 32 starts: 12 wins, 7 seconds, 3 thirds

  • Major wins: Arima Kinen (1988, 1990), Yasuda Kinen (1989), Mile Championship (1989)
  • Honors: Japan Racing Association Hall of Fame (2004)
